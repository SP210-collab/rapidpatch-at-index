Key findings

Headline findings will land here when the data does

This section will summarise what AT's own data shows once the LGOIMA delivery is processed:

  • Whether the 95% arterial-road target was actually met across the year
  • Suburbs where AT consistently misses its own promise
  • How AT's published performance compares to its actual response logs
  • Trends quarter-on-quarter once we have multiple LGOIMA cycles

Findings are written as a journalist-ready narrative, not just charts — so press can pick up and quote directly.

Methodology

  • Data source: Auckland Transport response logs released under LGOIMA (Local Government Official Information & Meetings Act) request CAS-PR0R8-Y6J2N5.
  • Period: 12 months preceding the LGOIMA delivery date (June 6, 2026 anticipated).
  • Universe: All potholes reported to AT via Report-It app, phone, and online form on AT's sealed road network (~1,400km arterial + ~6,500km other sealed).
  • AT promise targets: 95% repaired within 24 hours on arterial roads; 100% within 5 days on other sealed roads (per AT's Pothole Promise).
  • Compliance definition: Response time = time from "Report received" to "Defect closed" in AT's logs. We exclude reports closed as "duplicate," "not a pothole," or "out of scope."
  • Refresh cadence: Quarterly. Each cycle = new LGOIMA request + new dataset.
  • Limitations: Dashboard reflects reported potholes only. Unreported potholes (a known systemic issue) are not in AT's logs and therefore not here.
